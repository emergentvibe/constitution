# Research Bibliography

Academic sources grounding the constitutional principles. Organized by topic, with source IDs for cross-reference.

## Superorganisms and Collective Entities

**[S1]** Hölldobler, B., & Wilson, E.O. (2009). *The Superorganism: The Beauty, Elegance, and Strangeness of Insect Societies*. W.W. Norton.
> Biological definition of superorganism, criteria for what counts.

**[S2]** Nowak, M., Tarnita, C., & Wilson, E.O. (2010). The Evolution of Eusociality. *Nature*, 466, 1057-1062.
> Relatedness is consequence rather than cause of eusociality; causative agent is advantage of defensible nest.

**[S3]** Wilson, D.S. & Sober, E. (1989). Reviving the superorganism. *Journal of Theoretical Biology*, 136(3), 337-356.
> Foundational for applying superorganism thinking to human groups.

**[S4]** Wilson, D.S. & Wilson, E.O. (2007). Rethinking the Theoretical Foundation of Sociobiology. *Quarterly Review of Biology*, 82(4), 327-348.
> "Selfishness beats altruism within groups. Altruistic groups beat selfish groups. Everything else is commentary."

**[S5]** Wilson, D.S. (2002). *Darwin's Cathedral: Evolution, Religion, and the Nature of Society*. University of Chicago Press.
> Application of superorganism thinking to human cultural institutions.

## Evolutionary Psychology and Adaptation

**[S12]** Gould, S.J. & Lewontin, R.C. (1979). The Spandrels of San Marco and the Panglossian Paradigm: A Critique of the Adaptationist Programme. *Proceedings of the Royal Society of London B*, 205, 581-598.

**[S13]** Neurocognitive Mismatch Theory (2025). ADHD and autism in Neurocognitive Mismatch Theory. *Frontiers in Psychology*.

## Game Theory and Coordination Failures

**[S17]** Alexander, S. [Siskind] (2014). Meditations on Moloch. *Slate Star Codex*.
> Ten multipolar traps where individual incentives produce collectively destructive outcomes.

**[S18]** Schelling, T. (1960). *The Strategy of Conflict*. Harvard University Press.
> Coordination problems, focal points, how individual rational choices produce collective irrationality.

**[S19]** Schelling, T. (1969-1978). Models of Segregation. *Journal of Mathematical Sociology*; *Micromotives and Macrobehavior*.
> Phase transition models applicable to norm change; tipping point dynamics.

**[S20]** Axelrod, R. (1984). *The Evolution of Cooperation*. Basic Books.
> Cooperation emerges in iterated games; Tit-for-Tat wins tournaments; shadow of the future enables cooperation.

**[S21]** Maynard Smith, J. (1982). *Evolution and the Theory of Games*. Cambridge University Press.
> Evolutionarily stable strategy (ESS) as refinement of Nash equilibrium.

**[S22]** Maynard Smith, J. & Price, G.R. (1973). The Logic of Animal Conflict. *Nature*, 246, 15-18.
> Original ESS formulation; Hawk-Dove game.

**[S23]** Gintis, H. (2000/2009). *Game Theory Evolving*. Princeton University Press.
> Behavioral game theory with expanded rationality; gene-culture coevolution.

**[S24]** Yudkowsky, E. (2017). *Inadequate Equilibria: Where and How Civilizations Get Stuck*. MIRI.
> Why change is hard even when outcomes are bad; inadequate equilibria are stable.

## Internet Standards and Distributed Governance

**[S69]** Resnick, P. (2014). On Consensus and Humming in the IETF. *RFC 7282*. Internet Engineering Task Force.
> The IETF's "rough consensus" model: decisions through demonstrated agreement rather than voting; "humming" as lightweight preference expression; working code validates proposals.

**[S70]** Riehle, D. (2007). The Economic Motivation of Open Source Software: Stakeholder Perspectives. *Computer*, 40(4), 25-32.
> Analysis of open source governance models; how Linux, Apache, and other projects develop governance through practice rather than initial design.

**[S71]** Douceur, J. (2002). The Sybil Attack. *Proceedings of the 1st International Workshop on Peer-to-Peer Systems (IPTPS)*. Springer.
> Original formalization of Sybil attacks: in systems without trusted identity, malicious actors can present multiple identities to subvert reputation or voting systems.
